# Pawsitive Haven - Step-by-Step Adoption Guide

## Our Adoption Philosophy

At Pawsitive Haven, we believe every dog deserves a loving forever home. Our adoption process is designed to ensure great matches between dogs and families. We're not looking for perfect adopters - we're looking for committed ones.

## Step 1: Browse Available Dogs

### Where to Find Our Dogs

- **Website:** www.pawsitivehaven.org/adopt
- **Petfinder:** Search "Pawsitive Haven"
- **Adopt-a-Pet:** Search "Pawsitive Haven"
- **Social Media:** @PawsitiveHavenRescue

### What's Included in Each Listing

- Photos and videos
- Age, breed (or best guess), size
- Personality description
- Good with: kids, dogs, cats (if known)
- Activity level
- Special needs or requirements
- Current location (foster home or shelter)

### Can't Find the Right Dog?

Email adoptions@pawsitivehaven.org with:
- What you're looking for (size, energy, temperament)
- Your household (kids, other pets, living situation)
- We'll notify you when a matching dog becomes available

## Step 2: Submit Application

### Application Requirements

**You must:**
- Be 21 years of age or older
- Have valid ID
- Have landlord approval (if renting)
- Be able to provide veterinary care
- Have secure, appropriate housing for a dog

### What We Ask

The application includes questions about:
- Your living situation (own/rent, house/apartment, yard)
- Who lives in your home (adults, children, other pets)
- Your work schedule and lifestyle
- Previous pet experience
- Your plan for veterinary care
- Why you're interested in adopting

### Application Tips

- **Be thorough** - incomplete applications delay processing
- **Be honest** - we're not looking for perfection
- **Provide references** - vet reference strongly encouraged
- **Check your email** - we'll respond within 24-72 hours

**Submit application at:** www.pawsitivehaven.org/apply
**Questions?** applications@pawsitivehaven.org

---

## Step 3: Application Review

### What We Look For

- Safe, appropriate living situation
- Landlord permission (if applicable)
- Plan for veterinary care
- Lifestyle compatibility with dogs
- Understanding of commitment involved

### What We DON'T Require

- A yard (many dogs thrive in apartments with proper exercise)
- Home ownership (renters welcome with permission)
- Previous dog ownership (first-time owners welcome)
- Staying home all day (reasonable work schedules fine)

### Review Timeline

- **Standard applications:** 24-72 hours
- **Weekend submissions:** May extend to Monday
- **Complex situations:** May require additional discussion

You'll receive an email when your application is reviewed.

---

## Step 4: Phone or Video Interview

### Purpose

A brief conversation (15-20 minutes) to:
- Discuss your application
- Answer any questions you have
- Learn more about your lifestyle and what you're looking for
- Ensure good match between you and available dogs

### What to Prepare

- Have your application details fresh in mind
- Prepare questions about the dog(s) you're interested in
- Be ready to discuss your daily routine
- Have landlord contact info handy (if renting)

### Scheduling

After application approval, you'll receive a link to schedule your interview or a team member will reach out directly.

---

## Step 5: Meet and Greet

### Scheduling

After your interview, you'll be connected with the foster family to schedule a meet and greet.

**Contact:** meetandgreet@pawsitivehaven.org

### What to Expect

- Meet at foster's home, neutral location, or adoption center
- Spend time interacting with the dog
- Ask the foster questions about the dog's personality, routine, quirks
- Bring all family members (including resident dogs, if applicable)

### Meeting Tips

- Let the dog approach you (don't rush them)
- Get on their level
- Bring treats (ask foster first)
- Ask honest questions - we want the right match
- It's okay if it's not a match - another dog is waiting

### Resident Dog Meetings

If you have a dog at home:
- Meet on neutral territory (park, parking lot - not your home)
- Both dogs on leash with separate handlers
- Parallel walk before face-to-face meeting
- Keep it short and positive
- Multiple meetings may be needed

---

## Step 6: Home Check (If Required)

### When Home Checks Are Required

- Dogs with specific needs (secure fencing, no stairs, etc.)
- Applicants with less pet experience
- When recommended by foster or adoption team

### What We Look For

- Safe environment for a dog
- Secure yard/fencing (if applicable to the dog's needs)
- Appropriate space
- Removal of hazards

### What We DON'T Require

- A spotless house
- Expensive dog supplies
- A finished yard
- Luxury accommodations

**The goal is safety, not perfection.**

### Scheduling

Home checks are scheduled after successful meet and greet.
Contact: homechecks@pawsitivehaven.org

---

## Step 7: Adoption Approval

### Approval Criteria

All of the following must be met:
- Application approved
- Successful interview
- Positive meet and greet
- Home check passed (if required)
- Foster and adoption team agreement

### If You're Approved

You'll receive an email with:
- Confirmation of approval
- Next steps for scheduling adoption
- Estimated fees
- Required documents

### If It's Not a Match

Sometimes a specific dog isn't the right fit. This doesn't mean you're not a good adopter. We'll:
- Explain our concerns
- Suggest alternative dogs that may be better matches
- Keep your application active for future dogs

## Step 9: Adoption Appointment

### What to Bring

- Valid photo ID
- Adoption fee (or payment method)
- Collar and leash (or use the one provided)
- Crate or seatbelt harness for safe transport
- All adult household members should be present to sign

### What You'll Receive

- **Your new dog!**
- Adoption contract (signed copy for your records)
- Medical records and vaccination certificate
- Microchip registration information
- Certificate of adoption
- Welcome packet with resources
- Small bag of the dog's current food
- Pawsitive Haven collar and ID tag

### The Adoption Contract

By signing, you agree to:
- Provide proper veterinary care
- Keep ID tag and microchip registration current
- Not transfer the dog without Pawsitive Haven approval
- Return the dog to Pawsitive Haven if you cannot keep them
- Allow follow-up check-ins

---

## Step 10: Taking Your Dog Home

### The Ride Home

- Secure dog in crate OR seatbelt harness (never loose)
- Keep car calm and quiet
- Go directly home - no stops
- Have water available if long drive

### The First Day

- Keep it calm and quiet
- Show them their potty spot
- Set up their safe space (crate or quiet room)
- Let them decompress
- No visitors, no introductions to everyone at once

### Refer to First-Time Foster Guide

The "First 72 Hours" and "3-3-3 Rule" sections apply to adopters too! Your new dog needs time to adjust.

---

## Step 11: Two-Week Check-In

### What to Expect

An adoption team member will reach out at the two-week mark to:
- See how things are going
- Answer any questions
- Provide resources if needed
- Celebrate your success!

### Common Two-Week Concerns

| Concern | Usually Normal? | Action |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Still having accidents | Yes - adjustment period | Keep consistent routine |
| Not showing full personality | Yes - still decompressing | Give more time |
| Some anxiety | Yes - new environment | Provide routine, patience |
| Not eating much | Monitor - could be stress | Contact if >48 hours |
| Bonding with one person | Common in first weeks | Will likely expand with time |

### Need Help Sooner?

Don't wait two weeks if you're struggling! Contact us anytime:
- Email: followup@pawsitivehaven.org
- Behavior questions: behavior@pawsitivehaven.org

## Pre-Adoption vs. Foster-to-Adopt

### Pre-Adoption (Standard)

- Full commitment to adopt
- Dog goes home after adoption appointment
- Two-week trial period (can return if truly not working)

### Foster-to-Adopt

Available in certain situations:
- First-time dog owners wanting to ensure good fit
- Dogs with special needs requiring adjustment period
- When adoption team recommends

**How it works:**
- Foster the dog for 2-4 weeks
- Pawsitive Haven covers medical costs during trial
- If it's a match, complete adoption
- If not, dog returns to foster program (no judgment)

**Request foster-to-adopt:** Discuss with adoption team during interview

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does the process take?**
A: From application to adoption can be as quick as 5-7 days, or longer depending on scheduling and home check requirements.

**Q: Can I adopt if I rent?**
A: Yes! We need written landlord approval confirming pets are allowed.

**Q: Can I adopt if I work full-time?**
A: Yes! We'll discuss your schedule and ensure the dog's needs can be met.

**Q: What if I have kids?**
A: Many of our dogs are great with kids! We'll match you with an appropriate dog. Children should always be supervised with dogs.

**Q: Can I adopt if I already have a dog?**
A: Yes! We'll arrange a dog-to-dog meet and greet to ensure compatibility.

**Q: Why do you charge adoption fees?**
A: Fees help cover the significant cost of veterinary care, food, supplies, and rescue operations. Our fees are well below the actual cost of preparing a dog for adoption.

**Q: What if I change my mind?**
A: We have a two-week trial period. After that, you can still return the dog to us at any time - just contact us first.
